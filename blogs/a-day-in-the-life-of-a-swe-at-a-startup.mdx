#### 10:00 - 12:00 PM: Meetings, Meetings, Meetings

<img src="https://i.imgur.com/N3zzbAp.png" />

For the next two hours, I'm in meetings, dealing with different areas of the team.

- **Daily Standup (30 min)**

  Everyone saying, "Good on my end," and moving on... jk. This meeting is mainly about surfacing blockers and resolving them. It moves pretty fast, sometimes wrapping up in under 15 minutes.

- **Curriculum Bi-Weekly (30 min)**

  Today's focus: deciding on our next course. The winner? **Node.js** (dropping soon üëÄ). I'm on the Eng team, but I sit in Curriculum team's meetings since we're a small team.

#### 11:00 - 12:30 AM

Heading back home, showering, and doomscrolling Twitter for 20 minutes. So far, some of y'all might think that I don't have a life outside of work. While that is mostly true üòÖ, it's not as bad as it seems, mainly because all of this is a game to me.

What do I mean by this? By nature, I'm a very competitive person. Whatever I get into, I aim to be the best at. Whether that's becoming valedictorian (before my dropout phase üòÇ), reaching the top 30 in Overwatch, or now, the startup life. Startups represent the peak of competitiveness. Every single day, you're competing to be better than everyone else and better than the day before. 

Each new thing we accomplish is like a checkpoint in a game for me. Whether that's releasing a new product feature, completing [pre-seed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venture_round#:~:text=A%20pre%2Dseed%20or%20angel,discover%20initial%20product%2Dmarket%20fit.), or just surviving another day. Each one of these is a dopamine hit, and in a startup environment, there's always a next checkpoint. So from my point of view, I'm just a addict playing an endless game.

#### 12:30 - 3:00 AM
