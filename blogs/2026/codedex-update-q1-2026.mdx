## Daily Challenges

![Daily Challenges](https://i.imgur.com/PKSRlhd.gif)

SOOO excited to test out Codédex's newest feature ✨**[Daily Challenges](https://codedex.io/daily-challenge)**✨. 

In March, we invited our entire community to solve **one coding problem a day**. Think Wordle. Think Daily Crossword. But for technologists lvling up together. There’s something incredibly special about this format: everyone wakes up to the same challenge. A shared ritual.

This was also a _massive_ undertaking on the eng side – building out infrasturucture, 5 test cases, leaderboards, as well as curriculum's side, to ship a new problem every single day. But we did it!

### March Monthly Challenge

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/RHklaStF8T8?si=xohl_idcSRbQqoaW" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" referrerpolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" allowfullscreen></iframe>

To celebrate the launch, we paired it with the [March Monthly Challenge](https://www.codedex.io/community/monthly-challenge/f6fcKs3xRKWhJQdlkKGV).

This generated 1,512 community posts, a Monthly Challenge record (previously set in January with "30 Days of X"). So many people actually completed all 30 days, which is wild.

### Results & Next Steps

![March Monthly Challenge results](https://i.imgur.com/65tFrLg.png)
<Caption text="Data visualizations were brought to you by Alan @gntalan." />

While the people that loved Daily Challenges _really_ loved them, the growth didn’t compound the way we expected. WWhen we dug deeper, we found a massive drop-off: 54.6% of the participants were one-and-done. Which raises a few important questions for us:

- **Day-1 Retention:** How do we get users to come back on Day 2?
- **Acquisition:** How do we get the word out about Daily Challenges?
- **Sharing:** How can we make users want to share their results (aka make them look good)?

So... we're pausing Daily Challenges briefly, while we rethink a few things. Don't worry, it's not going away.

## Course Prereqs, Time to Complete, # Learners

![Preqs](https://i.imgur.com/2DNFBu1.png)

We continued improving our course landing pages based on the questions y’all ask via Intercom and in the community.

We added three new pieces of info:

- **Prerequisites:** Courses you should take beforehand.
- **Time to complete:** Estimated hours to finish the full course.
- **+### Learners:** Total number of learners who have started the course.

Small but BIG features. They make the learning journey much clearer.
