## Builds v1.0

![Daily Challenges](https://i.imgur.com/PKSRlhd.gif)

We're SOOO excited to share that we tested out a new Codédex feature: ✨**[Daily Challenges](https://codedex.io/daily-challenge)**✨. 

In March, we invited our entire community to solve **one coding problem a day**. Think Wordle. Think Daily Crossword. But for technologists lvling up together. There’s something incredibly special about this format: everyone wakes up to the same challenge. A shared ritual.

This was also a _massive_ undertaking on the engineering side – they built out the infrastructure, leaderboards, and test cases for both Python and JavaScript. On the curriculum side, we handcrafted a new challenge every single day. It was _a lot_, but we did it!

ICYMI, here are the Daily Challenges from March:

- **3/02:** [Wordle 🟩](https://www.codedex.io/daily-challenge/2026-03-02)
- **3/03:** [Blood Moon 🩸](https://www.codedex.io/daily-challenge/2026-03-03)
- **3/04:** [Holi 🌈](https://www.codedex.io/daily-challenge/2026-03-04)
- **3/05:** [Homebrew Computer Club 🤓](https://www.codedex.io/daily-challenge/2026-03-05)

### Results & Next Steps

![March Monthly Challenge results](https://i.imgur.com/65tFrLg.png)
<Caption text={<>Data visualizations were brought to you by Alan <a href="https://codedex.io/@gntalan">@gntalan</a>.</> } />

While the folks that loved Daily Challenges _really_ loved them, the growth didn’t compound the way we expected. When we dug deeper, we found that 54.6% of the participants were one-and-done, which raises a few questions about day-1 retention, beyond acquisition and sharing.

So... we're pausing Daily Challenges briefly, while we rethink how to bring it back better than ever. It's not going away, don't worry. 🫶

- 🕰️ **Time to complete:** Estimated hours to finish the full course.
