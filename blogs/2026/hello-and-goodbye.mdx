## How (the heck) did I get here?

It all started in December 2024. I had just graduated from undergrad at Baruch College as a marketing management major. As a creative, I naturally entered the world of freelancing social media/content creation. 🤳

In January 2024, I had mass applied to a bunch of companies and saw that Codédex was hiring a part-time Content Creator who can work out of the Dumbo, Brooklyn office. It all seemed perfect to me: I could still freelance with other companies, work in an office (I’m an ESFP-T, I need to socialize), and it was what I loved to do: content creation. I had no coding knowledge and barely a tech background, but hey… I’m always up for a challenge.

As part of the job application, I had to post a video on IG and I got this response from Sonny:

I interviewed with Sonny, the vibes were good, and this was my immediate reaction (I’m a very sentimental person 🥹):

The next week, I went to the office for a second round, an in-person interview with Lillian, our Head of Operations. I remember so vividly that I went in for a handshake and Sonny went in with a hi-five. I’m a ~professional.~ I met the team and everyone seemed super awesome and hardworking. 

A few hours later... I got a call from Lillian that I got the role!!! 🥳

## My Journey

After being a part-time Content Creator for about a year (February 2024 to December 2024), I decided I wanted to commit to really building out this awesome learn-to-code platform. I was honestly so inspired by everyone’s energy and passion for lvling up the next generation of technologists. That alone got me to stay and continue building with this awesome team.

It’s still a small (but mighty) group, and seeing how much this program has shaped our growth and how much love Codédex has received because of it… it has been incredibly rewarding.  💪🏻

## My Takeaways

### Building

Building at an early-stage startup like Codédex is an experience I’ll forever be grateful for. You have to *really* believe in vision, pivot quickly because things are ever-changing, and uplift those around you always because it is NOT for the weak. 

I’ve learned that experimentation and trusting your gut is truly the way to go. It’s how we grew Codédex from 250K to 1.5M registered users in the span of a few months. We launched v1 with four creators, v2 with six creators, and now v3 with ten creators. We trusted the process, hired awesome creators, and now am happy to say we’ve assembled a team of (content) avengers. 

I’ve also learned that building community means so much more than going viral. Of course, a 1m+ views skit video will always feel rewarding, but a 150k+ views video that tells a story and shows team personality is something that’ll resonate with people that much more. I’ll always remember when we launched v1 on Product Hunt last year and the way our community showed up for us is something I’ll never forget. We went on IG Live to do a Q&A, did some silly things for views, emailed, did a raffle on Discord and we were holding strong at top 3.
